Meeting & Onboarding Guide

Everything you need for the callback appointment after a plumber tries the demo.

Overview

What this meeting is

The plumber already heard the demo. They're interested. This call is where you understand how they work, answer their questions, and set up the free trial. You do NOT need to sell — the demo already did that. You're here to configure and launch.

1 — Meeting Agenda

What to cover (keep it under 15 minutes)
  • Ask what they thought of the demo
  • Collect business info (name, hours, services, area, dispatch fee)
  • Ask how they currently manage jobs/schedule
  • Ask what carrier they use (AT&T, T-Mobile, Verizon, other)
  • Ask: iPhone or Android?
  • Explain how call forwarding works (30 seconds)
  • Ask their preferences: all the time or after hours only? how many rings?
  • Get their emergency contacts (2 required)
  • Explain the 7-day free trial — no card, no commitment
  • Tell them you'll text them when it's live (same day)

2 — Collect Business Info

What you need from them
Ryan: "Alright, I'm going to set this up for you today. I just need a few things so she sounds like she's actually working at your shop."

Ask one at a time:

  • Business name (exactly how they answer the phone)
  • Hours of operation
  • Services they offer
  • Service area (cities/counties they cover)
  • Dispatch / service call fee (e.g. "$89 for the visit")
  • Emergency contact #1 — phone number for transfers
  • Emergency contact #2 — backup

Then ask:

Ryan: "How do you currently keep track of your jobs? Like, do you use Google Calendar, an app like Jobber, or just write it down?"
Most solo plumbers use: Google Calendar, pen & paper, Housecall Pro ($59/mo), or Jobber ($39/mo). ServiceTitan = never for solo guys. Some use nothing at all.

If they ask "how does she handle urgent calls?"

Ryan: "If someone calls about a gas leak or carbon monoxide, the receptionist tells them to leave the building and call 911 immediately. That's built in and can't be turned off — it's actually what the law requires, so we have to have that in place. For flooding, the receptionist walks them through shutting off the water. And everything else gets scheduled normally."
This is NON-NEGOTIABLE. Gas/CO → 911 advisory is hardcoded. Under the voluntary undertaking doctrine (Restatement §323), once the AI engages with a caller in distress, a duty of care attaches. The plumber cannot opt out of this — their instruction to "just take a message" does NOT eliminate YOUR liability to the caller. Your ToS already covers this.

3 — Call Forwarding Setup

Explain it simply
Ryan: "So here's how it works — you keep your same number. We set up something called call forwarding on your phone. When a call comes in and you can't get to it, after a few rings it forwards to our receptionist. She picks up, handles the call, and texts you everything. Your customers never know the difference."

Ask their preferences:

Ryan: "Do you want her answering all the time as a backup, or only after hours — like after 5 PM and on weekends?"
Ryan: "And how many rings do you want before she picks up? Some guys want 4-5 rings so they have time to grab it, some want her to jump in faster."

Two modes:

Mode A — Always-on backup (conditional forwarding)

Phone rings X times. If plumber doesn't answer → AI picks up. If plumber answers → AI never touches the call.

Ring count control: AT&T/T-Mobile: 5 to 30 seconds (1-6 rings). Verizon: not adjustable. For more than 6 rings → use Twilio-in-the-middle (Mode B setup).

Mode B — Twilio in the middle (time-based / custom rings)

Plumber forwards ALL calls to Twilio. Twilio rings the plumber's real phone for as long as they want (7, 8, 10+ rings). If no answer → AI picks up. Can also do time-based: during business hours → ring plumber first. After hours → AI immediately.

Use this when: plumber wants 7+ rings, or wants after-hours only, or is on Verizon and wants custom timing.

Carrier codes (send via text after meeting):

AT&T

*61*[number]*11#

Custom delay:
**61*[number]*11*[sec]#
(5/10/15/20/25/30)

Disable: ##61*11#

T-Mobile

**61*1[number]#

Custom delay:
**61*1[number]*11*[sec]#

Disable: ##61#

⚠️ T-Mobile codes fail on some plans. May need to call carrier.

Verizon

*71[number]

Ring count: NOT adjustable

Disable: *73

⚠️ iPhone iOS 18+: must turn OFF Live Voicemail first! Settings → Phone → Live Voicemail → OFF
MVNOs: Cricket = AT&T codes. Metro = T-Mobile codes. Straight Talk = depends on SIM network. US Cellular = *92 then number.
Fallback for ANY carrier: call carrier and say "I want to forward unanswered calls to [number]" — works 100%, takes 2 minutes.
Check if forwarding is on: *#61# (AT&T/T-Mobile only). Emergency disable ALL: ##002#

4 — Job Delivery Method

How the plumber gets call details
Ryan: "After she takes a call, how do you want to get the details? Most guys just want a text with the name, number, address, and what's wrong. But if you use Google Calendar or an app, we can push it straight in there too."

Option A — Text message (default, works for everyone)

Retell webhook → Make.com → Twilio SMS to plumber. Example text: "New call: John Smith, (404) 555-1234, 123 Oak St, kitchen sink leak, wants tomorrow morning"

Option B — Google Calendar

Retell webhook → Make.com → Google Calendar event with all details. Plumber sees it on their phone calendar.

Option C — Jobber / Housecall Pro

Retell webhook → Make.com → Jobber/HCP API → creates new job request in their system.

Option D — Pen and paper

Just text them. They write it down like they always do. Don't overcomplicate it.

5 — Technical Setup (you do this after the call)

Setup checklist — ~15-20 minutes
  • Duplicate Sarah demo agent in Retell
  • Update welcome message: "Hi! [Business Name], this is Nola, how can I help you?"
  • Add AI disclosure: "This call is recorded and handled by an AI assistant."
  • Swap in client's business name, hours, services, dispatch fee, area
  • Set emergency contacts (2 required)
  • Buy dedicated Twilio number ($2/mo)
  • Connect Twilio number to Retell agent
  • Set up Make.com webhook → SMS (or calendar/app based on their preference)
  • If Mode B (Twilio in the middle): set up Twilio Studio flow with time-of-day routing + custom ring timeout
  • Test end-to-end: call the number, verify AI answers, verify SMS/calendar delivery
  • Text plumber the forwarding code for their carrier
  • If Verizon iPhone: remind them to turn off Live Voicemail
  • Confirm it's working: "Try calling your own number from another phone and don't pick up"

7 — Common Questions & Objections

Pricing & Cancellation

"What happens after the 7 days?"

Ryan: "$99 a month. No contract, no commitment, month to month. You can cancel anytime."

"What if I want to cancel?"

Ryan: "Just text me. I turn it off same day. And you can disable the forwarding yourself in 10 seconds with one code on your phone — you're always in control."
Technical Questions

"Can I see the calls she took?"

Ryan: "You get a text after every single call with all the details. And I send you a weekly summary — how many calls she caught, how many jobs booked. Can you do that with your current setup? Most guys just have voicemail and half the time they don't even check it."

"Can I listen to the recordings?"

Ryan: "Can you listen to your calls right now? No, right? Neither can most plumbers. What you get is a full summary of every call — name, number, what's wrong, when they want it done — texted straight to your phone the second the call ends. You know everything without sitting there listening to recordings."
Only offer actual recordings if they push hard: "If you ever want to hear a specific call, text me and I'll send it over."

"What if she gives wrong information?"

Ryan: "It's impossible for her to say anything wrong. She only works with YOUR information — your hours, your services, your prices, your service area. Nothing else. She doesn't guess, she doesn't improvise. She's the best model on the market right now. And that's exactly why we go through all your info together — so everything is locked in from day one. If you ever want to change something, text me and I update it the same day."

"What if your system goes down?"

Ryan: "Our system runs at 99.99% uptime — that's less than one hour of downtime per year. But even if something ever did happen, your phone just works like it normally does. The call stays on your phone, rings you directly. Nobody loses a call."

"What about Spanish-speaking callers?"

Ryan: "She speaks Spanish too. If someone calls in Spanish, she switches automatically — handles the whole call in Spanish and still texts you the details in English. So you won't lose those calls either."
Spanish must be enabled in the agent settings (multilingual mode). Test it before promising. Georgia is ~11% Hispanic, higher in Atlanta metro.

"Can she actually schedule a specific time?"

Ryan: "She asks the customer when they'd prefer and what's going on, then texts you the request. You decide if that time works and call them back to confirm. That way you're always in control of your schedule — she's not booking things you can't do."
Calendar integration (Google Calendar) is possible later as an upgrade. For now, Sarah collects the request, plumber confirms. No double-booking risk.
Voicemail Conflict

CRITICAL: Carrier voicemail can steal calls before forwarding kicks in

The plumber MUST disable carrier voicemail or extend the voicemail timer past the forwarding timer. Otherwise voicemail picks up before the receptionist does.

Ryan: "One quick thing — we need to make sure your regular voicemail doesn't grab the call before our receptionist does. I'll walk you through turning it off, takes 10 seconds."

Disable voicemail by carrier:

AT&T

##004#

Disables all conditional forwarding including voicemail

Full removal: call 611 or 1-800-331-0500

T-Mobile

##004#

Same code works on T-Mobile

Full removal: call 611 or (877) 746-0909

Verizon

*73

Disables forwarding to voicemail

Full removal: call 1-800-922-0204

Fallback for ANY carrier: call carrier and say "I want to turn off my voicemail" — works 100%, takes 2 minutes.
⚠️ IMPORTANT: Disable voicemail FIRST, then set up call forwarding. If done in wrong order, voicemail may still intercept.
